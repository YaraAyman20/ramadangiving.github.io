When Sarah Ahmed first joined Ramadan Giving as a volunteer in 2022, she never imagined that decision would transform her life. Three years later, she leads our youth ambassador program, inspiring dozens of young people to discover the joy of service.

The Beginning

"I was looking for something meaningful to do during Ramadan," Sarah recalls. "A friend told me about Ramadan Giving, and I signed up to help pack food packages. I thought I'd do it once and move on. I was wrong."

That first experience left a lasting impression. Seeing the direct impact of her work—families receiving essential supplies, children's faces lighting up—ignited something in Sarah that wouldn't fade with the end of the holy month.

"I came to give, but I received so much more. The gratitude in people's eyes, the sense of purpose, the community I found—it changed my perspective on everything." — Sarah Ahmed

Growing Into Leadership

Sarah's dedication didn't go unnoticed. By 2023, she was coordinating volunteer teams for our lunch bag distribution program. Her natural ability to connect with people and organize complex logistics made her invaluable to the organization.

"Leadership wasn't something I sought out," she explains. "But when you care deeply about something, you naturally want to do more, to make it better, to involve others."

The Youth Ambassador Program

Today, Sarah heads our Youth Ambassador Program, mentoring young volunteers aged 15-25. The program has grown from 12 participants in its first year to over 40 active ambassadors.

  • Weekly volunteer opportunities during Ramadan
  • Leadership training workshops
  • Community service projects throughout the year
  • Mentorship and career guidance

Advice for New Volunteers

Sarah's message to anyone considering volunteering: "Just start. Don't wait for the perfect moment or the perfect role. Show up with an open heart, and everything else will follow. The community you'll find and the person you'll become are worth every moment."
